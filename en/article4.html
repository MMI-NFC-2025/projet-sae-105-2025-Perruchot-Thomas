Thelma & Louise

"I don't remember ever seeing women on screen who weren't sex objects or victims..."
— Geena Davis

This cult film by Ridley Scott (1991) features two women who, faced with male violence, choose to flee forward rather than submit. It resonates particularly with the struggle of forgotten women scientists: the refusal to stay in the place assigned to them.

Trailer

Movie Gallery

Why this link?

Like Nettie Stevens whose discovery was attributed to a man, Thelma and Louise suffer from a system that does not listen to them. Their revolt is a powerful metaphor for the struggle for recognition.
