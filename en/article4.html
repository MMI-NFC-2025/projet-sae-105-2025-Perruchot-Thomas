Thelma & Louise: A Cry for Freedom

More than just a road movie, Ridley Scott's film became a feminist anthem upon its release in 1991. A story of friendship and emancipation that echoes Nettie Stevens' journey.

Synopsis

Two friends, Thelma and Louise, decide to leave their monotonous lives for a weekend getaway. But their journey takes a tragic turn when Louise shoots a man who attempts to rape Thelma. What starts as a break becomes a flight across the American West, chased by the police.

Why this choice?

Like Nettie Stevens in the scientific world, Thelma and Louise try to exist in a world dominated by men. Their refusal to submit and their thirst for freedom make them powerful symbols of emancipation.

Music

The soundtrack, featuring Hans Zimmer and songs like "The Ballad of Lucy Jordan", perfectly accompanies this frantic race. It reinforces the feeling of urgency and the beauty of the vast open spaces.
